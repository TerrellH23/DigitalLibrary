A photographer named Michelle V.Agins is one of the longest serving staff photographers at the New York Times. She is the second only black woman to join the newspaper as a staff photographer where she has spent much of her career documenting black stories and Black American life. Hearing her story about her work life is like and her path to becoming a professional photographer is so inspiring. She realized that news photography could provide evidence and tell important in black neighborhoods. She witnessed the violence in her community and decided to photograph it. She found a way to tell stories of suffering through imagery.

The safety of pregnant women should be the top priority in a company during these terrible times. In the state of Georgia, a pregnant nurse was forced to attend to covid-19 patients. She felt she could still work, but she needed to have the right accommodations to keep working under these circumstances and her employer denied her. After denying her, she asked if she could be transferred and her employer denied her that. She was left with a terrible decision to quit. There is no law that protects pregnant women during this epidemic. According to the National Women’s law center, more than 1 in 5 pregnant workers are employed in low-wage jobs. Pregnant women also encounter prejudice and rigidity that leads to immediate and long term economic harm.
